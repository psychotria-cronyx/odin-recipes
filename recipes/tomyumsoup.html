Authentic Tom Yum Soup

Tom Yum is a quintessential Thai soup known for its bold and aromatic flavors. This hot and sour soup typically features fresh ingredients like lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and a variety of proteins, often shrimp or chicken. Its distinctive taste comes from a perfect balance of spiciness, acidity, and herbal freshness, making it a favorite both in Thailand and around the world.

The soup is not just a dish; it’s a celebration of Thailand's culinary heritage, embodying the country’s love for vibrant flavors. Often enjoyed as a starter or a light meal, Tom Yum is best served hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and sometimes chili paste for an extra kick. Its versatility allows for variations, but the authentic version captures the essence of Thai cooking.

Tom Yum Soup Ingredients

  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger if you can't find it at your local store)
  • 5-6 Kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 250g of shrimp, cleaned and deveined
  • 200g of mushrooms, sliced (straw or oyster mushrooms are optional)
  • 3-4 Thai bird's eye chillies, smashed (adjust to your heat preference)
  • 2-3 tables of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of palm sugar (brown sugar as alternative)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Optional: chilli paste (Nam Prik Pao) for additional flavor

Method

  1. Prepare the Broth
    • In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the smashed lemongrass, galangal (or ginger), and torn kaffir lime leaves.
    • Let it simmer for about 5 to 10 minutes to infuse the flavours. NOTE: You can go a little longer if you prefer a more intense flavour - be sure to taste periodically to achieve your desired balance
  2. Add Shrimp and mushrooms
    • Once the broth is aromatic, add in your shrimp and sliced mushroom. Cook until the shrimp turns pink and the mushrooms tender, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Season the Soup
    • Add the smashed bird's eye chillies, fish sauce, lime juice and sugar.
    • Taste and adjust seasoning as required: what your aiming for is a good balance of salty, sour and spicy flavours
  4. Serve
    • Remove from heat and discard the lemongrass and galangal slices
    • Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot. Optionally, add a spoonful of chili paste for extra depth and flavour

As a flavorful entrée, Tom Yum Soup pairs beautifully with many Thai dishes, including the spicy and aromatic Green Curry Chicken or an authentic Pad Thai Delight. For those looking to make it a heartier meal, consider adding rice noodles and extra protein, transforming it into a satisfying main dish.

Delight in the vibrant flavors of this authentic Tom Yum Soup, where the heat of fresh chilies meets the refreshing notes of lemongrass and kaffir lime. Each spoonful offers a harmonious blend of spicy and sour, making it a perfect starter or light meal that embodies the essence of Thai cuisine.
