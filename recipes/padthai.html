Authentic Pad Thai (Stir-Fried Noodles)

Description

Pad Thai is a quintessential Thai stir-fried noodle dish that harmoniously balances sweet, sour, and savory flavors. This vibrant dish features rice noodles tossed with shrimp or chicken, tofu, and a medley of fresh vegetables, all enveloped in a tangy tamarind sauce. Topped with crushed peanuts, bean sprouts, and lime wedges, Pad Thai is not just a meal; it’s an experience that showcases the rich culinary heritage of Thailand.

Originating from street food vendors, Pad Thai has become a beloved dish both in Thailand and around the globe. The perfect combination of textures and flavors makes it a versatile choice for any occasion, whether enjoyed as a quick lunch or a comforting dinner. Each bite is a celebration of Thailand's vibrant culture and love for bold flavors.

Pad Thai Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g shrimp or chicken, sliced
  • 100g tofu, diced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (palm sugar preferred)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Crushed peanuts, for garnish
  • Fresh lime wedges, for serving
  • Cilantro, for garnish

Method

  1. Prepare the Noodles
    • Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes, or until they are softened but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the Protein
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add the shrimp or chicken and cook until they are just cooked through, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Scramble the Eggs
    • Push the cooked protein to one side of the pan, pour the beaten eggs into the other side, and scramble until just set.
  4. Add the Noodles and Sauce
    • Add the soaked noodles to the pan, along with the fish sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, and lime juice.
    • Toss everything together to combine and heat through for about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add Vegetables
    • Stir in the bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for another minute until they are just tender.
  6. Serve
    • Transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates, garnish with crushed peanuts, cilantro, and lime wedges. Serve hot.
